Public Outreach & Volunteer Work

3D Theatre host/presenter, Origins Institute, McMaster University
(2011 to present)

Addressing audiences of 100+ people at a time, I present a suite of 3D movies and images in a show titled "Extreme Alien Worlds: A 3D Voyage Through and Beyond Our Solar System" that discusses the extreme environments present in our solar systems and places that might harbor life. In simple terms I explain how scientists go about searching for other planets and take questions from the audience.

Planetarium host/presenter, W.J. McCallion Planetarium, McMaster University
(2009 to present)

In coordination with the planetarium directors, I give both private and public shows at the McCallion planetarium. Typical one-hour private shows involve a tour of the local solar system, some discussion of Western constellations, an exploration of deep sky objects, as well an introduction to some of the latest developments in astronomy and astrophysics. Public shows are themed and mine have focused on the intersection between astronomy and culture. I give about 1-4 private shows per month, and about 2 public shows per year. My public show on August 25, 2010, was featured in a Hamilton Spectator article titled ‘In a basement at Mac we see creation’.

Webmaster & Executive Board, Engineers Without Borders (EWB)
(2005 to 2008, Columbia University Chapter)

The Columbia chapter of Engineers Without Borders is one of the most active student chapters of the organization. This chapter maintains three large ongoing overseas projects in developing communities of Ghana, Uganda, and India. I served as a member of the executive board and maintained the website for the Columbia chapter of EWB, then oversaw the transfer of the website to the incoming leadership.

﻿NanoDay in New York
(2007)

Volunteered at a public education outreach event at the City University of New York (CUNY), which featured Bill Nye and Nobel laureate Horst Stormer, and sought to get high school students excited about nanotechnology.
